Interesting Facts About Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  • When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months.

Interesting Facts About Chameleon

  1. Their toes work like salad tongs.
  2. They mainly change color to communicate and regulate body temperature.
  3. Their eyes can swivel around in two directions simultaneously.
  4. Chameleons can see in both visible and ultraviolet light.

Interesting Facts About Flying-Cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  3. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
  4. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.

Interesting Facts About Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. They are one of only a small number of species to pass the ‘mirror test’ – a test of self recognition.
  3. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  4. They use a range of skills, such as using the sun as a guide and an internal ‘magnetic compass’.

Interesting Facts About Bear

  • Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals.
  • Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing.
  • Bears are good at climbing and swimming.
  • There are only eight living species of bears,A group of bears is called a sloth.

Interesting Facts About Panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.
